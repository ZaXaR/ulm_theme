Lithium ores.
Description
Lithium (Li) – is the lightest metal and the least dense solid chemical element belonging to the alkali metal group.

Lithium and its compounds have several industrial applications, including heat-resistant glass and ceramics, lithium grease lubricants, flux additives for iron, steel and aluminium production, lithium batteries, and lithium-ion batteries.

Generally, lithium ores include other elements, like tantalum, niobium, beryllium, rubidium, caesium, sometimes tin, tungsten and others. Feldspar, quartz and mica, permanently found in lithium ores deposits, and may be industrially used.

The growing need in lithium resources causes a rush dedicated to research of new localization places of a third element.

Hoping to find previously unknown lithium stocks, a big range of companies are searching for it in the most distant corners of the globe.

Lithium mining in the world
The world resources of lithium are around 13 million tonnes.
Lithium mining and manufactring is conducted in the USA, Canada, the UK, Germany, Japan, Chily, Brazil, China, Russia and Australia.
The figures regarding the world lithium deposits widely range.

Types of consumption
  • Lithium minerals (24% of consumption) – are used in the products of glass and ceramics. They contribute to energy saving in the production of aluminum, plastics and rubber products.
  • Lithium chemicals (76% of consumption) – are used in batteries production, pharmaceutical industry and other areas. Lithium batteries are lighter then other types of batteries, and can hold a higher charge much longer compare to other batteries types.
Main lithium

Glass and ceramic industry are the biggest lithium consumers, account to around 31% of total consumption.

The second sphere of lithium consumption is production of batteries – 23% of the market (in comparison with 6% in 2000).

World lithium consumption grew from 12,800 to 22,600 tonnes since 2000 to 2010.

The popularity of lithium is explained by its unique electro-chemical potential and low coefficient of thermal expansion.

According to US Ministry of Energy research, the demand for lithium, that is used lithium-ion batteries in production will reach 28 000 tones till the end of 2030.

The large car makers predict persistent growth of the hybrid and electric cars sector. It is believed to reach a market share of 10-30% to 2020.

Nowadays lithium-ion storage batteries cover 10% of energy needs of electric and hybrid cars production.

It is easy to predict that the demand for lithium batteries will only grow.

Other spheres
of consumption
  • Textile industry (textile blanching).
  • Food industry (food-preservation).
  • Pharmaceutical industry (make-up production), lithium salts are used in medicine as antidepressants.
  • Lithium carbonate Li2CO3 is used in aluminum production.
  • Lithium alloys with magnesium, scandium, copper, cadmium and aluminum are new promising materials in aviation and space exploration.
  • Ceramics is produced based on aluminate and lithium silicate. It is used in military technics, metallurgy and thermonuclear energy.
  • Lithium strengthens lead alloys and makes it more flexible and resistant to corrosion.
  • Lithium stearate (“lithium soap”) is used as a high-temperature lubricant.
